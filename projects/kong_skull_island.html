Technical Terminology

List of terms used within this case study that are not layman's terms parlance
Endpoint: A specific URL or web address where a developed asset or data can be accessed and interacted with.

The Problem

Warner Bros. Pictures faced the challenge of creating a high-impact moment that could resonate on social media for the launch of their film, “Kong Skull Island”.

The Investigation

The optimal solution was clear – an impactful and attention-grabbing installation of a giant monkey head in our activation space, complemented by a social media campaign shared onto our giant clocktower screen. This concept garnered unanimous support upon suggestion, prompting us to begin the process of documenting and building out the sales pitch, complete with in situ mock-ups something JCDecaux was renowned for.

The Development

Task allocation became imperative for the project’s execution. My primary responsibility revolved around both project managing and collaborating with developers to establish a social media approval platform and develop a lightweight live-updating asset for the clocktower screen which could handle the developed endpoint for this campaign. So not only did I help front-end develop the assets and use the end points but I managed the development and requirements of the campaign.

The Solution

The solution involved first wrapping the entire station in promotional advertising, transforming SXS Melbourne into a kong-advertising spectacle. A colossal prop skull was crafted and strategically placed within the station in the most travest spot which happened to be near our flagship screen showing the social media strategy. The result exceeded expectations, culminating in a highly successful campaign that translated into a captivating and impressive video.
