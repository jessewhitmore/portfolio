Technical Terminology

List of terms used within this case study that are not layman's terms parlance
API: Application Programming Interface - A set of rules allowing different software applications to communicate with a system to retrieve specific data.
WebSocket: A protocol for real-time, bidirectional communication between clients and servers over a single connection.
CORS: Cross-Origin Resource Sharing - A browser security feature managing cross-origin HTTP requests for web applications.

The Problem

Faced with the immense competition of Black Friday, Amazon sought not just to participate but to redefine online purchase in the Australian market as the recent newcomer. Eager to make an indelible mark, the challenge was to craft a first-to-market initiative that would allow Amazon the opportunity to ensure their brand was as trusted in Australia as it is abroad.

The Investigation

Faced with the challenge of showcasing Amazonâ€™s reputation for competitive pricing, where prices could not be supplied for use in out-of-home as the company lacked direct control over prices; and contending with stringent legal requirements for accurate pricing and stock levels, an idea was born. We planned on leveraging the Amazon API to dynamically fetch real-time stock levels and precise pricing information. To guarantee accuracy, a timestamp mechanism was proposed, cross-referencing the data with the internal time of the ad units. It was my belief that enough safety measures could be implemented to assure that Amazon strict legal requirements could be met to ensure an accurate campaign.

The Development

Collaborating closely with developers, the challenge was to devise a method for data polling within the constrained environment of a JCDecaux digital screen. These screens operated on a paired-down Linux box without a live browser, rather launching a saved HTML file directly from the desktop, posing substantial CORS (Cross-Origin Resource Sharing) issues. The ingenious solution involved utilising WebSocket-based chat protocols to inject a JavaScript file housing a manifest and updated price list. In my role as a project manager, I ensured all legal requirements were met for Amazon, incorporating fail-safes like a default message in case of manifest polling issues and skipping out-of-stock products. Adding a layer of client flexibility, a bespoke system was established, allowing them to change the showcased product on the fly during the campaign.

The Solution

This campaign was groundbreaking for Amazon, a solution which was a world-first for Amazon. The live pricing fetching, a pioneering achievement for their company, not even performed within the more logical digital advertising space. All other providers had been unable to reach the high bar set by the Amazon team. The green light for this unique initiative attests to my ability to navigate complexities, push boundaries, and build confidence in others to deliver an unprecedented and impactful campaigns.
